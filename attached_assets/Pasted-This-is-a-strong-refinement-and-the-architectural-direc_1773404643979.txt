Now I want the next refinement pass to focus on three higher-level product controls so the prototype moves from “correct architecture” to “executive-grade financial supervision”:

1. Exception visibility
The main dashboards should not only show the pipeline.
They should also surface exceptions clearly, such as:
- operations stuck too long in review
- items returned for correction more than once
- final-approved items still pending ERP export
- ERP-posted batches with missing imported reports
- branches with repeated rejection patterns

2. Aggregation logic by module
ASAB ultimately exports each module as a consolidated accounting-style output for ERP.
Because of that, the prototype should begin showing not only operation-level lifecycle, but also module-level aggregation states such as:
- ready for export
- partially approved
- blocked by pending items
- exported
- ERP reports imported

3. Owner-facing final visibility
Once ERP reports are imported back into ASAB, the prototype should start distinguishing between:
- finance/internal review layer
- owner-facing reporting layer

The owner-facing layer should feel like a final clean read-only reporting destination, not another operational dashboard.
